# Plan 1 — "Mulaqat" (working title)
### Real-life friendships first. Curated events, ice-breaker games, and connections that start offline.

> **One-liner:** Timeleft for India — a personality-matched social events app where like-minded strangers meet over dinners, run clubs, and game nights, play built-in ice-breaker games so conversation never dies, and can connect (or even date) after the event.

**Working title options:** Mulaqat (मुलाक़ात — "a meeting"), Adda, Mehfil, Baithak, TableFor6. Pick after a trademark + App Store search.

---

## 1. The Problem

- India's metro cities are full of young people who moved away from home for work — Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi NCR, Pune, Hyderabad. They have colleagues, not friends.
- Dating apps (Tinder, Bumble, Hinge) are exhausting: endless swiping, ghosting, and pressure. People want low-stakes, real-life ways to meet.
- Meetup-style events in India are either too large and anonymous (100-person mixers) or too niche. Nothing curates **small groups of compatible strangers**.
- Even when strangers do meet, the first 20 minutes are awkward. Nobody solves the "what do we talk about" problem — that's our wedge.

## 2. The Insight (why we win)

Timeleft proved the model globally (6 strangers, one dinner, every Wednesday) and has entered India — but it's a thin experience: you book, you show up, you're on your own. We differentiate on three things:

1. **Games at the table.** Built-in ice-breakers, trivia, and party games designed for the group — the app is *alive during the event*, not just before it.
2. **Events beyond dinner.** Run clubs, board-game nights, chai meetups, treks — cheaper entry points for a price-sensitive market.
3. **Connection after the event.** A Hinge-style "connect" layer — but you can only match with people you've actually met. Offline-first dating is the safest, most natural dating there is.

**Positioning:** friendship app first, dating app second. In India this ordering matters — "meet new people over dinner" is socially acceptable to tell your parents and friends; "I'm on a dating app" still carries stigma for many. The dating layer is a quiet, opt-in bonus.

## 3. Target Audience

| Segment | Description |

## 5. Core Features

### 5.1 Personality Matching (the brain)
- 5-minute onboarding quiz: values, humor style, energy level (introvert/extrovert), interests, languages spoken, food preferences (veg/non-veg/Jain — essential in India), relationship intent (friends only / open to dating).
- Matching algorithm composes groups of 5–6 that balance: shared interests (so there's common ground) + complementary personalities (so there's spark) + language comfort + age band (±4 years).
- Every event feeds data back: post-event ratings ("who would you meet again?") continuously improve matching.

### 5.2 Curated Events (the heart)
- **Signature: Dinner for Six.** Every Wednesday & Saturday, 8 PM, partner restaurants. You book a seat, see only the neighborhood + vibe until 24h before, then get the venue + first names & fun facts of your table.
- **Run Club Sundays** — free/cheap entry point, sponsored by brands.
- **Game Nights** — board games & party games at cafés (huge in Bangalore/Pune).
- **Chai & Chill** — 1-hour low-cost weekday evening meetups.
- **Treks & Day Trips** — weekend format, higher ticket price.
- **Women-only tables** — critical trust builder for the Indian market.
- Host-led: trained community hosts (paid per event) anchor every event for the first year — quality control is the product.

### 5.3 In-Event Games (the differentiator)
When the event starts, the table "unlocks" a shared game room on everyone's phones (one person can also just read prompts aloud):
- **Ice-breaker deck** — tiered questions: Level 1 light ("what's your most controversial food opinion?"), Level 2 personal, Level 3 deep. The group votes to go deeper.
- **Desi Trivia** — Bollywood, cricket, 90s nostalgia, city-specific trivia; team-based, table vs. table on multi-table nights.
- **Two Truths & a Lie** — app collects everyone's entries during booking, reveals them at the table.
- **Hot Takes** — the app throws a spicy-but-safe debate prompt ("chai > coffee, defend your answer"), table votes.
- **Who's Most Likely To…** — anonymous voting, results revealed with laughter.
- Games are content — cheap to produce, easy to localize (Hindi/Hinglish decks), and they create the moments people post on Instagram.

### 5.4 Post-Event Connections (the retention engine)
- After an event, everyone rates the experience privately.
- **Connect:** send a friend request to anyone from your table. Mutual = chat opens.
- **Spark (opt-in dating):** if both people marked "open to dating" in their profile AND both send a Spark → a private 1-on-1 chat opens with a date suggestion ("You both loved the food debate — continue it over coffee?"). One-sided Sparks are never revealed. No swiping on strangers, ever — you can only Spark someone you've met.
- **Group chat:** the table gets an optional 7-day group chat to plan a round two ("same table next month?" one-tap rebooking).
- **Memory wall:** photos from the event, shared to the group.

### 5.5 Trust & Safety (non-negotiable for India)
- Mandatory selfie verification; optional government-ID verification badge (DigiLocker API) — verified badge shown on profile.
- Events only in vetted public venues; host present; live location share option.
- In-app SOS button + report/block; zero-tolerance one-strike policy for harassment.
- Women get female host contact before their first event.
- No last names, workplaces, or socials revealed until a mutual connection.

## 6. User Journey (first-time user)

1. **Download → quiz** (5 min, feels like a fun personality test, shareable result card: "You're a Warm Firecracker 🔥").
2. **Pick your first event** — city, date, budget band (₹/₹₹/₹₹₹), event type.
3. **Pay & lock seat** (UPI, one tap).
4. **T-24h:** venue revealed + table teaser ("Your table: 3 women, 3 men. Two engineers, one doctor, one founder. Four of you love standup comedy.").
5. **At the event:** check in via QR → table game room unlocks → host kicks things off → games flow.
6. **T+2h:** rate the night → send Connects/Sparks.
7. **Next morning:** mutual reveals + "book your next table" nudge with a returning-user discount.

## 7. UI / UX — Apple-grade design language

**Design north star:** the app should feel like it was designed by Apple — calm, confident, spacious. Think Apple Wallet + Apple Invites + Hinge, not a cluttered Indian super-app.

### Design principles
1. **One primary action per screen.** Every screen answers one question and has one obvious button.
2. **Whitespace is a feature.** Generous padding, large touch targets, no visual noise, no banner ads ever.
3. **Cards, not lists.** Events are full-bleed photo cards (like Apple's App Store "Today" tab) — swipe vertically through them.
4. **Native motion.** Spring animations, haptic taps on booking/matching, smooth shared-element transitions (card → event detail).
5. **Depth via blur.** Frosted-glass (glassmorphism) sheets over photos for event details — the Apple Music / iOS Control Center look.

### Visual system
- **Typography:** SF Pro (iOS) / a close grotesque like Inter (Android). Big, bold titles (34pt Large Title style), one type scale, no decorative fonts.
- **Color:** near-white/near-black base with **one** warm accent (terracotta or deep saffron — Indian-warm without being cliché). Full dark-mode support from day one.
- **Iconography:** SF Symbols style — thin, consistent line icons.
- **Photography over illustration:** real people, real tables, warm light. This sells the product more than any copy.

### App structure (4-tab bar, iOS style)
| Tab | Content |
|---|---|
| **Tonight** (home) | Your upcoming event card front and center (countdown, venue reveal, table teaser). Below: recommended events as large cards. |
| **Explore** | All events by category chips (Dinners · Runs · Games · Treks), calendar view, budget filter. |
| **People** | Your connections, pending Sparks, group chats. Hinge-quality chat UI — big photos, voice notes, date-idea prompts. |
| **You** | Profile, personality card, verification badges, event history ("12 dinners · 23 people met"), membership, settings. |

### Signature micro-moments (what people screenshot)
- The **venue reveal**: a card flip animation with haptic at T-24h.
- The **table teaser**: anonymized silhouettes that fill in as people check in at the venue.
- The **mutual Spark**: a subtle full-screen warm glow — celebratory but classy, no confetti spam.
- The **personality result card**: beautifully designed, watermarked, made for Instagram Stories (free top-of-funnel marketing).

## 8. Business Model & Monetization

### Revenue stream 1 — Event fees (day-one revenue)
- **Booking fee per dinner seat:** ₹299–₹499 (covers curation + host; food is paid directly to the restaurant by each guest — keeps our ticket price low and removes food-cost risk).
- Cheaper formats: Chai meetups ₹99, run clubs free–₹49, game nights ₹199, treks ₹999–1,999.
- **Dynamic pricing:** Saturday dinners and women-only tables can price higher; first event heavily discounted (₹99 intro offer).

### Revenue stream 2 — Membership subscription (the core engine)
| Tier | Price | What you get |
|---|---|---|
| **Free** | ₹0 | Book events at full price, 3 Connects per event, basic profile |
| **Plus** | ₹499/mo or ₹2,999/yr | 2 free event credits/mo, unlimited Connects, see who Sparked you first, priority seats on sold-out tables, advanced filters (age, language, profession) |
| **Concierge** | ₹1,499/mo | Everything in Plus + guaranteed weekly seat, premium venues, table-composition preferences, dedicated support |

Subscription framing: **"membership to a social club"**, not "premium dating features" — higher perceived value, less ick.

### Revenue stream 3 — Restaurant & venue partnerships
- We deliver 6 guaranteed covers on a weeknight (restaurants' deadest hours) — restaurants pay **10–15% commission on F&B** or a flat ₹150–250/head marketing fee, and offer our tables a fixed menu/discount.
- As volume grows: charge for **priority placement** (which restaurants get the Wednesday tables).

### Revenue stream 4 — Brand sponsorships & B2B
- Run clubs sponsored by athletic/wellness brands (shoes, electrolytes, athleisure) — brands pay per event or per city per month.
- Alcohol-adjacent & beverage brands for game nights (where legal), café chains for chai meetups.
- **B2B team-bonding:** companies buy private "Dinner for Six" formats for new-joiner cohorts — high-margin, uses the exact same product.

### Revenue stream 5 (later) — Spark upsells
- Once dating liquidity exists: "Second Chance Spark" (₹99, re-open an expired Spark), curated date-night packages with partner venues (commission).
- Never sell visibility boosts that corrupt matching quality — trust is the moat.

### Unit economics sketch (per dinner table of 6, Bangalore)
- Revenue: 6 × ₹399 booking = ₹2,394 + restaurant commission ≈ ₹900 → **~₹3,300/table**
- Costs: host ₹600 + payment/ops/support ≈ ₹500 → **~₹1,100/table**
- **Contribution ≈ ₹2,200/table (~65%).** 100 tables/week in one city ≈ ₹8.8L/month contribution before marketing — subscriptions and sponsorships stack on top.

### Monetization principles
1. Charge for the **experience and access**, never for safety features.
2. Keep first-event price near-impulse (₹99–199) — the product sells itself after one good dinner.
3. Push annual membership hard (cashflow + commitment = retention).
4. UPI-first checkout (Razorpay/Cashfree), no wallet top-ups, no dark patterns.

## 9. Go-To-Market

1. **Waitlist + scarcity launch** (Bangalore, Indiranagar/Koramangala first): "Tables are limited. Apply to join." Curation as marketing.
2. **Instagram-native content:** reels of real dinners (with consent), the personality card as a shareable, host POV content, "6 strangers, 1 dinner" series.
3. **Campus & corporate ambassadors:** free event credits for bringing 5 friends into the funnel.
4. **Partnership with existing run clubs / board-game cafés:** productize their community, split revenue — instant supply and distribution.
5. **PR angle:** "the anti-dating-app" / urban loneliness in Indian metros — media loves this story.

## 10. MVP Scope & Roadmap

### Phase 0 — Concierge MVP (Weeks 1–6, no app needed)
- Typeform quiz + manual matching + WhatsApp coordination + Razorpay payment links + printed ice-breaker card decks.
- Run 8–10 dinners in Bangalore. **Goal: validate that strangers show up, pay, and rebook.** Measure rebooking rate — if >30%, build the app.

### Phase 1 — MVP App (Months 2–4)
- Onboarding quiz, event booking + UPI payments, venue reveal flow, digital ice-breaker deck (Level 1–3 questions), post-event ratings + Connect, basic chat, selfie verification.
- One city, dinners + one cheap format (chai or run club).

### Phase 2 — Engagement (Months 5–7)
- Trivia & party-game engine (multiplayer game room), Spark dating layer, group chats + rebooking, Plus membership, host app/tools.

### Phase 3 — Scale (Months 8–12)
- Cities 2–3, restaurant partner portal, sponsorship packages, Concierge tier, Hindi/Hinglish game decks, B2B pilot.

### Suggested stack
- **App:** React Native (Expo) or Flutter — one codebase, iOS-quality animation achievable; native iOS later if funding allows.
- **Backend:** Supabase or Firebase (auth, Postgres, realtime chat, push) + Node/edge functions for matching jobs.
- **Payments:** Razorpay (UPI, cards, subscriptions).
- **Realtime games:** Supabase Realtime / Ably (shared game-room state).
- **Matching v1:** rule-based scoring (interests overlap + personality balance + constraints); ML later once you have outcome data.

## 12. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| No-shows kill table chemistry | Prepaid seats, T-24h confirmation, standby list, strike system |
| Gender imbalance at events | Women-only tables, women's pricing offers, verified-only tables, female hosts |
| Timeleft scales India faster | Out-localize: games in Hinglish, cheaper formats, UPI-first, dating layer they don't have |
| Safety incident damages brand | Verification, vetted venues, hosts at every event, SOS + one-strike policy, insurance |
| Dating layer alienates "friends-only" users | Spark is invisible unless both opt in; brand stays friendship-first |
| Price sensitivity | ₹99 formats as entry drug; membership priced under 2 coffee dates/month |

## 13. What "winning" looks like

Year 1: 3 cities, 400+ events/month, 20k MAU, ~₹40–60L/month gross revenue run rate, rebooking >35%.
Long-term: **India's default way to make friends in a new city** — the social graph that starts offline. The dating layer, B2B, and brand network are all built on that graph.
